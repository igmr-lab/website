Image-Guided Medical Robotics Lab

Active Projects

The IGMR Lab has multiple on-going projects related to medical robotics and imaging.

Robotic Optical Coherence Tomography

We develop autonomous robotic systems that acquire optically-stabilized optical coherence tomography (OCT) volumes over a large workspace while attenuating residual motion artifact. We explore this technique to expand access to OCT for screening as well as enable OCT imaging in new patient populations and clinical settings.

Draelos, “Mobile Robotic Optical Coherence Tomography System for Ophthalmic Imaging in Clinical Environments.” in IEEE International Symposium on Medical Robotics (ISMR), 2025, pp. 1–7, Best Paper Award – 2nd Place.
  • P. Ortiz, A. Narawane, R. P. McNabb, A. N. Kuo, J. A. Izatt, and M. Draelos, “Sensor-Driven Digital Motion Correction of Robotically-Aligned Optical Coherence Tomography Retinal Volumes.” Biomedical Optics Express, vol. 16, no. 4, pp. 1616–1637, Apr. 2025, Editor’s Pick.

Surgical Navigation in 3D

Live intraoperative 3D imaging unlocks new viewpoints for surgeons but quickly causes disorientation if surgeons' hand and viewed tool motions become misaligned. We develop the Arbitrary Viewpoint Robotic Surgery (AVRS) concept which introduces a robotic surgery system between surgeons' hands and tools that preserve performance by maintaining hand-tool alignment.

Draelos, “Dual-Arm Teleoperated Robotic Microsurgery System with Real-Time Volumetric OCT Image Feedback.” in IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS), 2025, pp. 21423–21430, 46% acceptance rate.
  • M. Draelos, B. Keller, C. Toth, A. Kuo, K. Hauser, and J. Izatt, “Teleoperating Robots from Arbitrary Viewpoints in Surgical Contexts.” in IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS), Sep. 2017, pp. 2549–2555, 45% acceptance rate.

Adaptive Imaging

Point- and line-scan imaging modalities, such as optical coherence tomography and beamsteering ultrasound, waste effort when scanning the background of sparse scenes. We explore approaches to online adaptive scanning that increase effective imaging throughput by focusing on regions of interest, rather than the full scene, using machine learning.

  • D. M. Rajani, F. Seghizzi, Y.-L. Lai, K. G. Buchta, and M. Draelos, “Dynamics-Aware Deep Predictive Adaptive Scanning Optical Coherence Tomography.” Biomedical Optics Express, vol. 16, no. 1, pp. 186–207, Jan. 2025.
  • M. Draelos, C. Viehland, R. P. McNabb, A. N. Kuo, and J. A. Izatt, “Adaptive Point-Scan Imaging beyond the Frame Rate-Resolution Limit with Scene-Reactive Scan Trajectories.” Optica, vol. 9, no. 11, pp. 1276–1288, Nov. 2022.

Soft Robot Face Mask

Standard-of-care face masks require continuous application of force and healthcare provider attention in order to maintain position over the nose/mouth and avoid large leaks. We are developing a soft robotic face mask for respiratory support that self-seals to the patient’s face without any provider intervention.

  • Y. S. Narayanan, Y. Ma, R. B. Gillespie, M. Draelos, and X. Huang, “Adaptive Self-Sealing Soft Robotic Face Mask (SRFM) with Particle Jamming.” in IEEE-RAS International Conference on Soft Robotics (RoboSoft), 2025, pp. 1–6, Best Student Paper Award Finalist.

Data Visualization

Densely-sampled volumetric datasets, like those produced with optical coherence tomography, present challenges in data visualization. We address such challenges in surgeon situational awareness and in virtual reality environments.

  • M. Draelos, B. Keller, C. Viehland, O. M. Carrasco-Zevallos, A. Kuo, and J. Izatt, “Real-Time Visualization and Interaction with Static and Live Optical Coherence Tomography Volumes in Immersive Virtual Reality.” Biomedical Optical Express, vol. 9, no. 6, pp. 2825–2843, Jun. 2018.

Vortex Open Source Project

We develop and maintain the vortex open-source software project to facilitate rapid development of new, experimental optical coherence tomography systems in C++ or Python. A growing community of academic research and industry startups rely on vortex to jumpstart their development process.
